Fire Prevention Month

About Fire Prevention Month

Fire Prevention Month is observed every March to raise awareness about fire safety and promote fire prevention practices. This month-long campaign aims to educate the public on the common causes of fires, the importance of smoke alarms, and the proper use of fire extinguishers. Fire Prevention Month is also an opportunity to teach children about fire safety and help families create a fire escape plan.

Fire Prevention Tips

General Safety Tips

  • Install smoke alarms on every floor of your home, inside and outside of bedrooms, and in hallways.
  • Test smoke alarms monthly and replace batteries every six months.
  • Keep flammable objects at least three feet away from heat sources.
  • Use caution when cooking and never leave cooking unattended.
  • Keep lighters, matches, and candles out of reach of children.
  • Have a fire escape plan and practice it with your family.

Electrical Safety Tips

  • Do not overload electrical outlets or extension cords.
  • Replace or repair damaged electrical cords and appliances.
  • Use light bulbs with the correct wattage for fixtures.
  • Do not use damaged electrical appliances.
  • Unplug electrical devices when not in use.
  • Have a licensed electrician inspect your home's electrical system every ten years.

Emergency Preparedness

Being prepared in case of a fire emergency is crucial to keeping your family safe. Here are some tips to help you and your family stay safe:

  • Create a fire escape plan that includes at least two exit routes from every room.
  • Practice your fire escape plan with your family regularly.
  • Teach your children how to call 911 in case of an emergency.
  • Designate a meeting place outside of your home where your family can gather in case of a fire.
  • Keep a fire extinguisher in an easily accessible location and make sure everyone in your family knows how to use it.
